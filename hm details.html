Hospitality Management

A hospitality management course teaches students the skills and knowledge needed to work in the hospitality industry. Students learn about the operations of hotels and other hospitality businesses, and develop skills in areas like management, marketing, and customer service.

Key Learning Areas:

  • Industry-specific knowledge: Learn about the different departments in a hotel, including the front office, housekeeping, food and beverage, and reservations.
  • Hard skills: Learn professional skills like room pricing, accounting, cost-control, and scheduling.
  • Soft skills: Develop skills like teamwork, leadership, design thinking, finance, budgeting, and marketing.
  • Communication skills: Develop verbal, written, and presentation skills.
  • Analytical skills: Develop analytical, critical, and problem-solving skills.
  • Negotiation skills: Develop negotiation and teamwork skills.
  • IT skills: Develop IT skills for collecting, analyzing, and presenting information.
  • Networking skills: Develop networking skills through discussion and debate with peers.

Entry-Level Jobs:

  • Hotel and restaurant management
  • Food and beverage service attendant
  • Bartender
  • Housekeeping attendant
  • Front office desk clerk
  • Storekeeper
  • Kitchen commis
  • Cabin crew
