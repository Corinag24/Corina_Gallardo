Cooperative Management Course

Cooperative management courses can cover a range of topics, including:

Key Learning Areas:

  • Theories and concepts: Students learn about the theories, models, and dynamics of cooperatives and rural institutions.
  • Management functions: Students learn how to plan, organize, lead, and control a cooperative.
  • Financial management: Students learn about the basic concepts of cooperative financial management, including accounting systems, financial statements, and budgeting.
  • Entrepreneurship and enterprise development: Students learn about these areas in the context of the cooperative sector.

Institutions Offering Cooperative Management Courses:

  • SMU: Offers a course that introduces students to the theories, concepts, and models of cooperatives and rural institutions.
  • CDA: Offers a 28-hour course for cooperative officers on the basic competencies required to oversee the financial resources of a cooperative.
  • CEM-UPLB: Offers a Master of Management major in Cooperative Management that provides students with advanced knowledge on management, entrepreneurship, and enterprise development.
  • Equip Africa Institute: Offers a Diploma in Cooperative Management that is assessed internally and externally.

Cooperative management is complex because it often involves managing the affairs of several different businesses under one structure.
