Midwifery Course

A midwifery course prepares students to provide care for women, infants, and families during pregnancy, childbirth, and the postpartum period.

Diploma in Midwifery

A two-year program that includes general education and professional courses, as well as clinical practicum. Graduates are eligible to take the government licensure examination.

Bachelor of Science in Midwifery (BSM)

A four-year degree program that covers topics such as:

  • Maternity care during pregnancy, labor, delivery, and OB-GYN emergencies
  • Post-partum care
  • Caring for infants and children
  • Interpreting diagnostic examinations
  • Assisting in a cesarean section procedure

Choosing a Midwifery Course

When choosing a midwifery course, ensure that it is approved by the Nursing and Midwifery Council (NMC) and leads to qualified midwife status.

What is Midwifery?

Midwifery is a health science and profession that involves:

  • Providing skilled, knowledgeable, and compassionate care
  • Working with women throughout their lives, including their sexual and reproductive health
  • Helping to reduce maternal and newborn mortality
